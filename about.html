History

Imus Institute is the oldest non-sectarian co-education private school in Cavite, offering a complete Academic Secondary Course recognized by the government. Founded by citizens led by Manuel and Lorenzo Paredes, the school aimed to provide affordable elementary and secondary education to the youth. Key officers and members of the Board of Trustee included Manuel C. Paredes, Lorenzo B. Paredes, Epifanio S. Paredes, Remedios Monzon-Paredes, and Benito V. Rementilla. An Honorary Board of Trustees was also established.

 

The Ymus Central Academy was established in 1923 as a non-stock family corporation under the Bureau of Commerce and Industry. Its formal opening began on June 12, 1923, and continued throughout the school year. The academy was chosen for its location in Imus, a town near Manila and one of the largest in Cavite, and its proximity to the capital city and the province's main transportation lines. The founders of the academy outlined their objectives and philosophy for the establishment of the school.

 

To help the parents of Imus and neighboring towns with limited means to give their children quality education; To instill in the minds of the students the irreversible truth that an individual is destined to achieve something to meet the exigencies of life; To make the students realize that success depend mainly upon their own untiring efforts so that they can discover for themselves their hidden talents, power and capabilities; and To attend strictly to the development of the mind and the molding of the character of the students.

 

Having found to its satisfaction, the efficiency of instruction, adequacy of the laboratory equipment in the science courses, the sufficiency of books in the library as well as the physical set-up of the school, then the Department of Instruction, through the Superintendent of Private Schools, Mr. Mcquaide, extended government recognition to the Imus Central Academy for its elementary, high school and vocational courses in the same year of its establishment. The original site of the school was the building of Pilar Lodge No. 15 (Free and Accepted Masonry) fronting the public plaza of Imus. In 1928, Dr. Eugenio Villanueva, Sr. took over the ownership and administration of the school, changing its name to Imus Institute and limiting its courses to Secondary Education. He lasted from 1929 to 1933. In 1934, he devoted his time to his prosperous medical profession and transferred ownership to a newly formed corporation, including the Board of Directors and Officers. They were:

 

Mr. Mariano Tirona - President | Dr. Lorenzo B. Paredes - Vice President | Mr. Melito Darvin - Secretary | Miss Fidela Buenaventura - Treasurer | Mr. Paulino Stuart - Member |

Dr. Leoncio B. Monzon, Dr. Enriqueta Virata, and Mr. Zosimo Topacio joined the group, serving as President and Chairman from 1945 until his death in 1980. The school operated until World War II, opening for one year during the Japanese occupation. After liberalization in 1945, the Imus Institute opened again, offering a complete High School course. The College Department, founded in 1952, closed in 1959 due to lack of enrollment.

 

The Imus Institute, established in 1950, moved to a 2,884 square meter lot on D.T. Nueno Avenue due to a growing post-war student population. Construction began in March 1951, and the first Board of Trustees meeting took place in August 1951. After 28 years in the Pilar Lodge No. 15 building, the institute began accepting college students in 1952. The curriculum included a one-year Secretarial course and a two-year Liberal Arts course, eventually leading to the Bachelor of Science in Commerce degree. The College Department at the University of the Philippines offers a wide range of degrees, including Associate in Arts, Certificates in Secretarial Science, Associate in Secretarial Administration, Two-Year Junior Secretarial Course, BS Commerce majors in Management, Accounting, Finance, Banking and Finance, and more. The school also provides distance education, including Master of Arts in Education, Social Development, Business Administration, Nursing, Public Administration, Doctor of Education, and Business Management, in collaboration with Phillipine Women's University.

 

Imus Institute built a four-story concrete building for fourth-year high school students in Poblacion, Imus, as part of its expansion and development program. The building was built after three of its main campus buildings were destroyed by fire in 2000. The Enriqueta Building was used for classes, but the institute continued its academic programs. The new M-Building was completed the following year. The High School Department, led by Architect Encarnacion N. Raralio, introduced computer-assisted learning in English, Mathematics, and Science. Additional buildings were added, including a covered wide stage, Science High School Building, and a rooftop for Physical Education classes. Starting 2006-2007, three secondary school curricula were introduced: Regular High School, Science and Adult High School, and Post-Secondary Certificate Course in Commercial Cooking. The Science High School curriculum caters to students pursuing Engineering and Science Education Programs, adding Advance Science, Statistics, and Mathematics with Computer Research in each year level.

 

Imus Institute offers Adult High School to those who missed high school courses, allowing them to complete necessary credits for a diploma. The institute is committed to quality education and producing competent graduates. To comply with the K-12 Basic Education Program (R.A 10533) in the Philippines, the school has changed its name to Imus Institute of Science and Technology. The new name focuses on strengthening 21st-century skills for secondary to tertiary learners. The academic curricula, strategic plans, and instruction are integrated with advanced sciences and technology to train value-laden graduates and citizens. Imus Institute aims to become one of the best educational institutions in the Cavite province and prepare for university status.
